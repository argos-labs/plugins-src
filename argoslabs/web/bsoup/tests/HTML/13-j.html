Contact Angle
When radial and axial loads are applied to a bearing, a contact angle forms between the balls and the inner and outer rings. Contact angle represents the displacement between the inner and outer rings. Larger contact angles support higher axial loads and lower radial loads.

Internal Clearance
Internal clearance is how much the bearing’s rolling elements move within the raceway before the bearing is mounted to a shaft. A greater internal clearance means there is more space, or looseness, within the bearing. Internal clearance is most commonly expressed with the letter C followed by a number, with C0 being standard clearance. Some systems display internal clearance with MC followed by a number, with MC3 being standard clearance. Bearings with a lower internal clearance are used to reduce noise and vibration while greater internal clearances are suitable for high-speed applications.
